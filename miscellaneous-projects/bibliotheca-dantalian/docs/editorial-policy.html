Editorial Policy

The house rules, covering naming, sourcing, spoiler scoping, and what counts as a fact on this wiki.

Contents

  1. Naming
  2. Sourcing
  3. Spoiler scoping
  4. Facts and theories
  5. Red links

These are the house rules for the Veilharbor Archive. Every page is expected to follow them, and pull requests are reviewed against them.

Naming

  • Character and term names follow the official English release. The Japanese name and romaji appear once, in the page's infobox, and after that the English name is used throughout.
  • A term with no official English rendering uses the romaji, italicized, with the Japanese in parentheses at first mention.
  • Page titles are the shortest unambiguous form: Iori Tatsumi, not "Iori Tatsumi (protagonist)".

Sourcing

  • Every page lists its sources in a References section, populated from the page's front matter.
  • Acceptable sources: the published volumes and episodes themselves, the official site, and the publisher's announcements. Interviews are citable with issue and date.
  • Fan translations, forum posts, and social media rumors are not sources. If a fact only exists in one of those, the wiki waits.

Spoiler scoping

  • A page's summary, infobox, and opening section must be safe for someone who knows only the series premise.
  • Plot beyond that lives in ::: spoiler blocks scoped to anime (aired episodes) or manga (published volumes ahead of the anime).
  • The scope label states its coverage plainly: "Anime spoilers through episode 13", "Manga spoilers through volume 11".
  • When the anime catches up to a manga beat, editors move the relevant block from manga scope to anime scope. The Anime page's episode table is the reference for where the adaptation currently stands.

Facts and theories

A fact is something a published chapter, episode, or official announcement states. Everything else is a theory, and theories appear only in clearly labeled Notes sections, if at all. Wording like "appears to", "is implied", or "fans believe" is a sign a claim should be cut or sourced.

Red links

Linking to a page that does not exist yet is encouraged; the link renders in amber and the build lists it as wanted. Red links are the wiki's to-do list. See How to Contribute for turning one into a page.
