Veilharbor is the port city where Lanternfall is set. Its defining institution is funerary: the city does not bury or burn its dead, it kindles them, one lantern per life, and hangs the lanterns along the Emberline.

The Emberline

The Emberline is the procession road that switchbacks from the harbor to the hilltop columbarium, lined with roughly nine thousand lanterns. Walking it end to end takes a full night, which is the point; the walk is the city's act of remembrance. The Wickkeepers' Guild maintains every flame on it.

The lighthouse

The harbor lighthouse predates the guild and sits outside its jurisdiction, one of the series' quietly load-bearing legal facts. Its keeper is Genzo Ukai; its apprentice is Iori Tatsumi. Unlike the Emberline's lanterns, the lighthouse flame was never a person, or so the guild's register says.

The Lanternfall

Roughly once a generation, lanterns across the city begin failing over a span of months. The city calls the event a Lanternfall. Flames rescued in time are carried home and rekindled; flames lost are lost. The newest Lanternfall begins in chapter 1.

Manga spoilers through volume 8

Volume 8 establishes that the current Lanternfall is at least partly artificial: some failures are thefts by The Palefinger dressed as natural ones. Whether any Lanternfall was ever fully natural is an open question the series has only started to circle.
